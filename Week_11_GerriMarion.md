## Your Reflection

When discussing the pilot testing of our project, I had to do a lot of reflecting on my own. Specifically, when it came to my sampling methods and how I had to rework those. 

I couldn't find that much of a sample size when I was first searching for my data. I wanted to focus on new players to *Stardew Valley*, or at least as new as possible to the game. I was okay including people who had little hours into the game or hadn't played in years joining in because there have been so many updates since its first release ten years ago. I was also focused on their first "100 Days in/of Stardew Valley" videos because that could tell me how far they truly got in their gameplays, if they completed it, what mechanics they lean towards, who they romance, etc. Both of those criteria led to a finicky sample size, and a majority of men who had never played *Stardew Valley* for the first time than women.

What I hadn't considered until yesterday is that the sample size is so specific that it limited quite a bit of results. I hadn't considered to look into "first playthrough" or "blind playthrough" or "first time" along with the game's title. I also hadn't thought to look at other games with similar mechanics, or as similar as possible. 

In the end, I found the dataset that I needed to not only work on my pilot testing, but the remainder of my project in general. The other game I chose is *Fields of Mistria* because not only is it still fairly new, as well as still being in early access, but returned similar results when paired with "first/blind playthrough" and "first time." It's inspired by *Stardew Valley* as well, which is something I noticed when first looking for a suitable game that hits all aspects. Most cozy games use *Stardew Valley* as their foundation when advertising, and *Fields of Mistria* is no exception.
