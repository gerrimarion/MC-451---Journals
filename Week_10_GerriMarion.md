## Your Reflection

When reading the chapter about the codebook, I started to think heavily about my variables and how I came to them. It makes sense that you'd want something as specific as possible, and that other rules may not come up until the official research begins. That's what happened with me when I started watching the intros to the YouTube videos. 

When I first started my research, I had to switch my 10 men and women to 5 masculine and feminine-presenting people because finding beginner players that presented as feminine weren't as likely. If they were feminine-presenting, they were attempting those challenges with additional modifiers or challenges in the game. Then, when I started to watch their introductions before diving into the gameplay, I figured out what I was wanting to research and the various gaming mechanics that go into Stardew Valley. 

I tried to be as specific as possible with my variables as someone who both plays and thoroughly enjoys the game, but I'm sure I missed out on things since it's still being updated for additional content. The biggest variable shift that I included is if players seek out additional, outside sources for help. It isn't uncommon for players to turn to the Wiki for guidance or schedules for characters, and I'm curious if that'll play into a person's overall completion of their "100 Days in/of Stardew Valley" videos.
