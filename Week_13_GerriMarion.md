## Your Reflection

During the lectures and through the in-class follow-along's, I enjoyed learning about the different ways you can insert charts and customize them as your own. I used to do a lot of that in Excel a couple of years ago, but I found it to be more interesting through RStudio. Even though it's still a fairly new program to me, and I'm still learning all the kinks and work arounds, it's pretty straight forward and a little more forgiving than most other programs. 

How RStudio handles visualizations, and the concepts of visualizations through datasets, makes a lot of sense and is easier to interpret overall. Yes, you have the raw data and the number corresponding to each code and decision rule, but that's about it. It's hard to read and tell even after it's cleaned up. By putting it in different visualization formats and comparing things through that way, it makes it a lot more straightforward and simpler to read.

I figure that I'll use the summarise and compare methods the most with my own dataset. Since I focused a lot of gender and the performance of gender through cozy games, I'll more than likely have to compare gaming mechanics and gender performance through masculine-presenting and feminine-presenting folks. I wanted to see if there were double or side-by-side bar charts as well, and I'm sure their are, but that may get overwhelming and confusing with the gaming mechanics option. I also would like to try and use the proportional chart for my own dataset when it comes to gender performance. Maybe it'll show how often people did perform their gender and which genders were more likely to do so. 

All in all, it'll be fun to play around with and see what looks the best and makes the most sense for my own datasets.
